The problem

Your charity deserves better than spreadsheets and duct tape

Most charity CRMs force you to choose between fundraising and service delivery. Alder CRM doesn't.

🗂️

Data lives everywhere

Donor info in one system, volunteers in a spreadsheet, case notes in a shared drive. Most charities still re-key data between disconnected systems.

💸

CRMs that cost too much

Enterprise CRMs can cost £10k+ a year. Even mid-range options charge over £100/month for basic features. 86% of UK charities have income under £500k — these prices don't add up.

🔧

Fundraising or services — pick one

Fundraising CRMs don't do case management. Case management systems don't handle donations. You end up buying two tools and syncing between them.

🔒

Locked into legacy systems

Migration can cost thousands and take weeks of retraining. Vendors know this, so they stop innovating once they've got you.

👻

Opaque pricing

"Contact us for a quote" means unpredictable costs. Feature gating means the tools you actually need are always on the next tier up.

⏱️

Admin eating into impact

UK charities spend an estimated 15.8 million hours a year on grant monitoring reports alone — roughly £204m in staff time (Plinth, 2025). That's money not reaching beneficiaries.

Who's behind Alder CRM

Built by someone who's been in the room

Alder CRM is built by James Inman, founder and trustee of neurobetter — a UK charity supporting neurodivergent mental health. He's spent years working at the intersection of technology, governance, and the charity sector, and spent over a decade building child protection and case management systems for schools.

This isn't a product built by people guessing what charities need from the outside. It comes from direct experience of running a charity, sitting on a board, managing safeguarding responsibilities, wrestling with compliance, and knowing that the existing tools don't quite fit.
